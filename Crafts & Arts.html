Healer Seave

A healer sieve is a small or large sieve made from hardwood with a fine mesh, used for sifting ingredients like flour, spices, and powdered sugar to remove lumps and impurities, resulting in smoother final products. It can also be used to strain seeds from juice, rinse grains, or prepare herbal teas, showcasing its versatility for both cooking and traditional remedies. Read more

Mokorotlo Hat

A mokorotlo is a traditional, conical-shaped straw hat of the Basotho people of Lesotho, serving as both a cultural symbol of the Basotho and the national emblem of Lesotho. Inspired by the shape of Qiloane Mountain, the hat is a national symbol, appearing on the Lesotho flag, vehicle license plates, and within the homes of Basotho people. Read more

Mopotjoane

A small clay pot: In Sesotho, a mopotjoane is a small, fire-baked clay vessel. It is used for carrying or storing various liquids, including traditional beer or sorghum juice. The term is a diminutive of nkho, the general word for a pot or jar.Read more

Mafielo

In Sesotho, a language spoken in Lesotho and South Africa, lefielo means "broom". The word is used to refer to both a common cleaning tool and a traditional broom made from thatching grass .Read more

Lipetja
