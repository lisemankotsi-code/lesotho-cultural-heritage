Traditional Dances & Instruments

Mokhibo

What is mokhibo dance?
The mokhibo is a women's dance performed on the knees, the body gently rising and falling as the hands are swept upwards. An informal choir stands behind the line of dancers, singing and clapping. The maqekha is a special dance that forms part of the first rites of girls' initiation

Litolobonya

What is Litolobonya?
Litolobonya is a form of song and dance exclusively for girls and married women. The girls' version of litolobonya is performed in the open, where everyone may watch, but the version for women is highly secretive and is performed only by those who have given live birth.

koriana

What is Koriana?
koriana is a Sesotho word for "accordion," and refers to a type of traditional accordion music popular among the Basotho people of Lesotho and South Africa.

Lithoko

What is Lithoko?
The Basotho associate and identify themselves through the use of their clan names, which specify their ancestral origins; namely the Bataung, Basia, Bafokeng, Makgolokoe and Bakuena et al. The royal family of Lesotho is largely made up of Bakuena.

Moropa

What is Moropa?
moropa is a type of drum and is central to the musical traditions of several ethnic groups like Basotho.

Lekolulo

What is Lekolulo?
Lekolulo is a type of flute traditional to Lesotho and associated with herding boys. It is one of several musical instruments used in the region, which also include the setolo-tolo and thomo.

Sekhankula

What is Sekhankula?
The mamokhorong, also known as the sekhankula, is a traditional Basotho musical instrument, a bowed monochord resembling a single-string violin.

Mmino oa lingoale

What is Mmino oa lingoale?
This is the music of women when they are being initiated.

Mohobelo

What is Mohobelo?
The more traditional mohobelo is a men's stomping dance that consists of synchronized movements and high kicks. Women perform their own dance by kneeling in a line and beating the ground with their knees

Mokopu

What is Mokopu?
Lipina tsa Mokopu is a Sesotho dance performed by girls that depicts songs and dances related to harvesting the mokopu (pumpkin) during the African New Year celebrations.

Sekupu

What is Sekupu?
Sekupu sa Basotho is a traditional drum used by the Basotho people of Lesotho and South Africa. It is made from a clay pot covered with a stretched animal skin, typically that of a goat. The drum is played with the hands.

famo

What is Famo?
"Famo" refers to a music genre that originated in Lesotho, a type of music that has become intertwined with violent gangs.

Setepe

What is Setepe?
Setepe is a traditional Basotho cultural dance from Lesotho, characterized by its joyful expression, often performed at weddings and festivals, and accompanied by music, which evolved from flutes to the accordion as the main instrument. It serves as an important form of cultural expression, fostering social cohesion and creativity within the community.

Lekope

What is Lekpe?
Lekope is a traditional Basotho mouth bow, a single-stringed musical instrument played by the Sotho people.

Thomo

What is Thomo?

In Lesotho, Thomo is a traditional instrument featuring a calabash resonator and a string made of horse hair, typically played by women to create music.

Ntlamo

What is Ntlamo?
A Basotho dance that incorporates stamping and is influenced by Zulu traditions

Mangae

What is Mangae?
Mangae is a traditional basotho music that they sing during the initiation.

Liphota

What is Liphotha?
Liphotha is a traditional dance perfomed by boys during special events in Lesotho.

Mokorotlo song

What is Mokorotlo?
Mokorotlo is a traditional song that is sang during the initiation of boys.

Senyamo

What is Senyamo?
It can refer to a type of traditional rhyming game played at a thakaneng, a traditional open-air dormitory for unmarried youth

Moqoqopelo

What is Moqoqopelo?
Moqoqopelo is a specific Sesotho-language dance and the title of several songs, including one by the Nigerian artist Nana Coyote and another by the group African Calabash

Sekebeku

What is Sekebeku?
Sekebeku is a traditional Sesotho idiophone, specifically a wooden jaw harp played by women and sometimes girls in Lesotho.

Pitiki

What is Pitiki?
Pitiki is a traditional Basotho practice that serves as a safe, empowering space for women, particularly after childbirth, to share knowledge, support each other, and celebrate their becoming
